Welcome to my fury simulator!

FAQ:

Q: Is this a spreadsheet?

A: No, this is not a spreadsheet. On the contrary, this is an attempt to create a actual simulator with world of warcraft classic mechanics.
Meaning that when the "Run simulator" button is pressed, a character is created, wearing the items specified in the drop down menus.
Then all the stats from the items are combined and the following values, which are used in the simulator, are computed:
- Total attack power
- Weapon skill
- Hit chance
- Haste rating
- Weapons extra hit probability
- Damage multipliers

Where the hit and crit are used to create the following hit tables:
- Main-hand white-hit table
- Off-hand white-hit table
- Two-hand white-hit table (queried for the off-hand when a heroic strike is queued)
- Yellow-hit table

In the next step a target is generated and the combat phase starts, where the simulator will start to swing the characters weapons towards the target.
Rage generated will be spent on abilities following a configurable ability-policy, and hits are generated by drawing random samples from the hit-tables.
This process is then repeated X times where data is sampled from each such simulation.

Q: Whats the difference between a simulator and a spreadsheet?

A: A spreadsheet performs no simulation! Instead a spreadsheet consists of a mash-up of expected values, where white-hit damage, rage, yellow hits,
flurry charges etc., are modelled as good as possible. But its nearly impossible to accurately model discrete events, such as cooldowns, flurry charges,
rage-capping, swing timer resets (extra hit), etc. with this approach, and the results might therefore be inaccurate. I believe people have done a great effort
in developing such models, but from the forementioned reasons they will never be as accurate as a simulator!

Q: What equations are used in this simulator?

A: The tables for crit, hit, glancing hits, and weapon skill have all been based on the results of the Attack table github.
Their models have been verified on classic servers and which I strongly recommend to check out.
I have derived models for rage myself, since the ones available online are inaccurate (try them yourself!).
To derive models I have sampled data myself, with different weapons, against different targets, and the model used now matches the sampled data perfectly.
